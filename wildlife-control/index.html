Common Wildlife Problems in Conway, AR and How We Can Help

Conway, Arkansas, with its mix of urban and natural landscapes, is home to a variety of wildlife that can occasionally cause issues for homeowners and businesses. Understanding the signs of wildlife intrusion is the first step in addressing the problem effectively. Here are some common wildlife problems encountered in the Conway area and how Conway Pest Pros provides expert solutions.

Nocturnal Noises in Attics or Walls

Hearing scratching, scurrying, or thumping sounds from your attic or within your walls, especially at night, is often a strong indicator of wildlife activity. Animals like raccoons, squirrels, and bats are frequently the culprits behind these unsettling noises. These animals seek shelter and warmth in protected areas of your home, particularly during colder months or when raising young. Not only are the noises disturbing, but these animals can also cause damage by chewing on electrical wiring, tearing apart insulation, and leaving behind waste that can lead to unpleasant odors and health hazards. Our service involves thorough inspection to identify the source of the noise, humane removal of the animal, and sealing of entry points to prevent future intrusions. We understand the urgency of addressing these nocturnal visitors and act swiftly to restore peace and quiet to your home.

Damage to Property

Wildlife can cause significant damage to the structure and contents of your property. This includes chewing on siding, fascia boards, and wires, tearing up insulation, and damaging gardens or landscaping. Squirrels are notorious for gnawing on wood and electrical wires, posing a fire hazard. Raccoons can damage roofs and siding as they attempt to enter attics or chimneys. Birds can build nests in vents, causing blockages and fire risks. Our team is experienced in identifying the type of damage caused by specific wildlife and implementing targeted removal and exclusion strategies to prevent further destruction. We can also recommend or perform minor repairs to address damage caused by wildlife entry.

Visible Wildlife on Your Property

Seeing wildlife frequently on your property, whether it's squirrels scampering across the roof, raccoons rummaging through trash cans, or birds nesting in inconvenient locations, is a clear sign that they are comfortable in your environment. While seeing wildlife from a distance can be enjoyable, their close proximity can be problematic, especially if they are becoming a nuisance or posing a risk. Our wildlife control services offer solutions ranging from humane trapping and relocation to implementing deterrents and modifying the environment to make it less appealing to these animals. We work to safely and effectively manage wildlife interactions on your property.

Concerns About Diseases and Pests

Wildlife can carry diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans and pets. Rabies, histoplasmosis (from bat guano), and leptospirosis are just a few examples of potential health risks associated with wildlife presence. Furthermore, animals like raccoons, squirrels, and birds can bring in other pests like fleas, ticks, and mites, further complicating the situation. Our wildlife control services include assessments of potential health risks and recommendations for sanitation and clean-up of areas contaminated by wildlife. We prioritize not only the removal of the animal but also the restoration of a safe and healthy environment for you and your family.

Addressing Common Misconceptions About Wildlife Control

There are many myths and misconceptions surrounding wildlife control that can lead to ineffective or even harmful attempts to address issues. Understanding the realities of professional wildlife management is crucial for making informed decisions and ensuring the safety of both your family and the animals involved. Let's debunk some common myths and highlight the importance of professional help in Conway, AR.

Myth: I can scare wildlife away with loud noises or bright lights.

While sudden disturbances might temporarily startle some animals, they are rarely effective long-term deterrents. Wildlife that has become comfortable near or inside your home is often motivated by strong instincts like seeking food, shelter, or a place to raise young. Loud noises or lights might cause them to leave for a short period, but they are likely to return once the disturbance stops, especially if the underlying attractants (like food sources or easy entry points) remain. Professional wildlife control focuses on addressing these root causes through removal and long-term exclusion, which is a much more sustainable solution than temporary scare tactics in Conway.

Myth: All DIY wildlife traps are safe and humane.

DIY traps purchased from retail stores may not always be appropriate for the specific animal you are dealing with, and their use requires careful consideration to be truly humane. Improperly used traps can injure animals or attract non-target species. Furthermore, handling trapped wild animals without proper training and equipment can be dangerous, as they may bite, scratch, or carry diseases. Professional wildlife control experts like those at Conway Pest Pros are trained in the humane use of traps, understand animal behavior to minimize stress, and have the equipment and knowledge to safely handle and relocate captured animals where appropriate. Relying on professional expertise ensures that wildlife is managed ethically and effectively.

Myth: Removing one animal solves the problem permanently.

Unfortunately, removing a single animal doesn't guarantee that another won't take its place, particularly if the attractive conditions on your property remain unchanged. If the entry points are still open and food or shelter is abundant, other wildlife may quickly discover and utilize the same resources. This is why the exclusion and prevention phase of wildlife control is so critical. Sealing entry points and making your property less appealing to wildlife is essential for long-term success. Our comprehensive service addresses not just the immediate animal but also the factors that attracted it in the first place, providing a more durable wildlife control solution in Conway, AR.

Myth: Wildlife issues are only a problem in rural areas.

While wildlife is often associated with natural environments, they are incredibly adaptable and frequently thrive in urban and suburban areas like Conway. Parks, green spaces, residential gardens, and even commercial properties can provide suitable habitats and food sources for various species. Raccoons, squirrels, opossums, and birds are common visitors to suburban neighborhoods, attracted by trash cans, pet food left outdoors, bird feeders, and accessible structures for shelter. No matter where you are located in Conway, if you have a property with potential food or shelter, you are susceptible to wildlife conflicts. Professional help is available for all types of properties in the Conway area, including residential and commercial properties, as well as neighboring cities such asLittle Rock,North Little Rock,Bryant,Benton, andMaumelle.

Myth: It's too expensive to hire a professional for wildlife control.

While there is a cost associated with professional wildlife control in Conway, AR, considering the potential costs of ignoring the problem or attempting ineffective DIY solutions, professional service is often a cost-effective investment in the long run. Wildlife can cause extensive damage to your property, including structural damage, electrical problems, and insulation destruction, which can result in significant repair bills. Furthermore, there are potential health risks associated with wildlife presence and improper handling. Professional services provide effective removal, prevent future issues through exclusion, and ensure the job is done safely and correctly, ultimately saving you money and headaches down the line. We offer competitive pricing and upfront estimates for our wildlife control services in Conway, AR.
